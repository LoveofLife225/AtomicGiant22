Isotopes and Atoms.

- Models -

The simplest atom you can make is the hydrogen atom. It is made up of one tiny positive proton, and a very tiny negative electron.

Because of their opposite polarity they are attracted to each other.

What happens if you add a neutron into the nucleus with the proton? What changes?

The charge doesn't actually change! The hydrogen atom just has more mass. You could say it's heavier.

Given that with this added neutron it acts very similarily to regular hydrogen we call it an isotope, a variation of the same atom.

But, this is just one way to view things. In Quantum Physics, it is found that these structures are actually made of quantum waves The same goes for all the things you see around. The table. The computers.
